About Me

I grew up in the ’80s and ’90s with computers (286, Amiga, 486). Naturally I was fond of gaming and went into computing and programming. During my college years, I coded in TI‑Basic on my TI‑82 calculator.

Discovering computer possibilities by exploring operating systems sparked my curiosity: BeOS (now Haiku), Plan 9. With Linux I was attracted to minimal desktop experiences and window managers (wmii, wmi, dwm, xmonad) – I loved their KISS approach. Thanks to that community I learned Golang and Haskell.

Sport was also part of my childhood. The 1986 World Cup left a mark – a victory against Brazil and a defeat against Germany. I played football for ten years; the team game taught me that no player is more important than the team. I eventually settled on midfield and even captained the side. Tennis was my second favourite sport – unlike football, the game isn’t timed, and you can still win a match by gaining points after points. It taught me focus and confidence.

Professionally I started as a Desktop Engineer, focusing on OS and application integration with tools such as Sysprep, ImageX and Wise Package Studio. I then specialised in software packaging, preferring InstEd, which deepened my understanding of the Windows Installer model. Communicating with Niel Hunt, the creator of InstEd, was a unique experience.

Scripting in VBScript and PowerShell became a core part of my job. I moved further into automation with Windows Installer XML (WiX) and built static‑packaging architectures that could absorb dynamic sources. This led me to found my own company to spread this new way of thinking. While many clients off‑shored mass integrations to keep costs low, WiX allowed us to embed integration knowledge directly into developers’ stacks.

Realising that desktop engineering was no longer my playground, I naturally gravitated toward DevOps. Cloud computing reinvented how we consume resources and introduced new methods. I dug into Azure DevOps SDK, ARM templates and PowerShell to build novel services, while also enjoying coding in React JS, TypeScript and Python.

My latest mission involved delivering a three‑tier solution (Golang, React JS, PostgreSQL) to simplify technical assessments for cloud‑migration projects – a challenging yet rewarding experience.

On the personal side, my wife and I are proud parents of a boy and a little girl.

Today I’m looking for a role that balances DevOps technologies (Docker, Kubernetes, etc.) with opportunities to sharpen my programming skills, especially in Golang and Htmx.

May 28 2024 – my EF SET score is 71.
